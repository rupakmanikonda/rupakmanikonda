# 💫 About Me:
"So, I’ve been a Solutions Engineer for over five years now, mostly focused on the Fintech and SaaS space. I’ve always been that person who sits right in the middle—translating what the business needs into actual, working API integrations.<br>+1<br><br>My work really covers the whole lifecycle. It usually starts with that technical discovery phase—literally sitting down with partners to map out their workflows. Like when I was at M2P Fintech, I was working directly with banks to integrate KYC and Video KYC APIs. In fintech, you can’t just 'connect' things; you have to worry about regulations and identity checks right from day one. In fact, for one of our partner integrations, the Video KYC logic I helped implement ended up catching enough bad actors to save them around $500K a month in potential fraud. That’s the kind of business impact I care about—it's not just making the API work, it's making sure it's secure.<br>+3<br><br>From there, I move into the actual design. I help partners structure their REST API flows, clean up their JSON payloads, and set up webhooks for asynchronous events. I also guide them through the security side, ensuring we’re aligned with compliance standards like PCI or SOC 2.<br><br>When it comes to implementation, I’m pretty hands-on. I’m usually in the sandbox alongside the engineering teams—running tests in Postman, validating payloads, and digging through logs to troubleshoot issues before we ever go live. I also run Proof of Concepts (POCs) just to make sure both the technical and business teams are confident in what we’re building.<br>+2<br><br>And even after launch, I usually stick around to act as that technical escalation point. I actually enjoy creating the integration guides and API documentation to make sure the next onboarding runs even smoother.<br><br>Basically, I don't just help close the deal—I focus on making sure the technology behind it is sound, compliant, and scalable for the long haul."
